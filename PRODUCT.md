# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary user is the **comfort-first upgrader**: a man aged 18–45 who has outgrown big-box, multipack underwear and wants to replace his everyday drawer with something engineered, not just bought. He isn't shopping for a single gift or occasion — he's a habitual replenisher who will keep coming back for restocks and multipacks once he's convinced. His context is ordinary: getting dressed for work, the gym, or a flight, not a special event. The job to be done is simple to state and hard to satisfy: *stop compromising on the one thing I wear every single day.*

## Product Purpose

ELKO exists to make premium, engineered men's underwear and everyday essentials (boxer briefs, trunks, briefs, undershirts, performance wear, multipacks) as considered as any other piece of a modern wardrobe. Success looks like a first-time buyer converting on a single pair, then returning within 60–90 days for a multipack — evidence that the comfort claim held up in daily wear, not just in photography.

## Positioning

Everyday essentials engineered with the same rigor and fabric science as premium outerwear — comfort you can feel in the stitching, not just claim in the copy.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: **Shop Now** (drives to full catalog). Secondary fallback: **Explore Collection** (for visitors not ready to buy — lets them browse by curated collection first).
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: *"Engineered for comfort. Designed for confidence."*
- Belief ladder: (1) this fabric is genuinely different from what I wear now → (2) the fit will actually hold up, not just look good in the photo → (3) other men like me already made this switch and didn't go back → (4) the risk is low (365-day guarantee, easy returns) → (5) Add to Bag.
- Proof on hand: animated social-proof counters (100,000+ customers, 4.9★ average rating, 500,000+ products sold), verified-purchase customer reviews with size/fit feedback, and a 365-day quality guarantee. No third-party press or case studies on hand yet.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, precise, confident. ELKO speaks the way a well-tailored garment fits — nothing shouts, nothing is left undone. Confidence comes from restraint: minimal branding, deliberate whitespace, and fabric claims stated plainly rather than hyped. The tone sits closer to CDLP and Calvin Klein's editorial cool than to Lululemon's warmth or a performance-sportswear brand's energy.

## Anti-references

Not loud, fast-fashion big-box underwear branding — no discount-bin visual clutter, no oversized sale-sticker badging, no primary-color multipack shrink-wrap aesthetic. Not a sportswear-energy brand either (no neon accents, no aggressive diagonal graphics); the performance line should still read as quiet luxury, not gym-branding.

## Design Principles

- Restraint reads as confidence: minimal branding and generous whitespace are the luxury signal, not gold foil or ornamentation.
- Every fabric claim earns its place through specificity (percentages, named processes) rather than adjectives alone.
- The path from browsing to Add to Bag should stay frictionless — quick add, quick view, and persistent cart context reduce hesitation without pressuring the visitor.
- Trust is built cumulatively: guarantee, verified reviews, and social proof should appear near the moments of highest purchase hesitation (product page, cart), not just on a dedicated page.
- Gold is a signal, not a wash — reserve the accent for the moment that matters (a badge, a CTA, a stat) rather than tinting large surfaces.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: minimum 4.5:1 text contrast, visible focus states on all interactive elements, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for animated sections (hero, counters, carousels), and keyboard-navigable menus, modals, and drawers. No additional named requirements beyond AA at this time.
